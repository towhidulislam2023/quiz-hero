Q2. Difference Between LocalStorage, Sessionstorage and Cookies?

LocalStorage

LocalStorage is a way for websites and web applications to store information on a user's device, such as their computer or phone. This information will be stored for an unlimited amount of time and can be accessed even after the user closes their browser or turns off their device. It's like having a small storage space on your device that a website can use to save information for later.

SessionStorage

SessionStorage is web storage available in modern web browsers. It's similar to LocalStorage, but with one key difference. While data stored in LocalStorage persists indefinitely until explicitly removed, data stored in SessionStorage is only available for the duration of the current browsing session.

Cookies

A cookie is a piece of information that a website sends to a user's web browser, which stores it on their computer or device. This information is usually in the form of a name-value pair separated by semi-colons. Cookies are used to remember a user's preferences or login information, so they don't have to enter it every time they visit the website. Cookies help maintain the state of a user and keep their information consistent across different web pages of the same website.
